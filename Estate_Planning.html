Assuring heirs are not overly taxed

When an individual receives a significant amount of money or property, they are likely subject to taxes on that income. There are ways to protect your beneficiaries from this asset transfer, but you need to think strategically to make it happen.

Protecting gifted assets from liquidation

If an heir to an estate receives assets, such as a home or business, that he or she is not prepared to manage and take responsibility for, he or she may be forced to liquidate the assets. Miami Dade & South Florida Trust Lawyer

Trust Lawyer

If you are caring for an individual who has special needs or cannot manage the receipt of financial assets on his or her own, you may need to set up a trust to protect his or her best interests. Different types of trusts are used for different purposes, from caring for minor dependents to caring for individuals with disabilities, and choosing the right option can be a complex undertaking.

By working with an experienced Miami trust attorney, you can better understand the options available for you and your loved ones and give yourself peace of mind, knowing that the people you care about will be taken care of should anything happen to you.

Special Needs, Inter Vivos And Pour-Over Trusts Attorney

At the law firm of Jorge L. Gurian, P.A., we take pride in offering our clients options for protecting themselves and their loved ones from the unexpected. We understand the sensitive nature of these issues, and balance our compassion with stability that remains grounded in what can actually be done under the law.

We handle a wide range of trust planning and administration issues, including those related to complex and simple trusts, such as:

Special needs trusts:

These are ideal for individuals who are leaving money or assets to a disabled individual and want to assure they do not disrupt the individual's right to receive government benefits.

Testamentary trusts:

This refers to a type of trust that is set up after the estate owner has passed away.

Inter vivos trusts:

Commonly called a living trust, this trust is set up while the estate owner is still alive.

Pour-over trusts:

These originate through a will, where the writer of the will turns over assets to a trustee upon his or her passing.

There are many advantages and disadvantages to each option as you set up a trust or deal with trust administration. We, at the law firm of Jorge L. Gurian, P.A., can walk you through the process and answer your questions personally.
